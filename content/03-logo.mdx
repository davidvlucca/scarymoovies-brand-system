# Logo

## Overview

The ScaryMoovies logo is a two-part system: a wordmark and an emblem that anchors it.

The wordmark sets "SCARY MOOVIES" in a custom distressed horror typeface. The letterforms carry a worn, hand-cut texture — edges that look torn rather than printed, strokes that have been roughed and degraded. The effect is deliberate: it reads as a title card from a film that has been run through a projector too many times. It is legible at a distance and unmistakably horror-genre, without relying on the tired visual shorthand of dripping blood or generic grunge. The distress is controlled, not chaotic.

The double "O" in "MOOVIES" carries a specific visual treatment. The negative space carved out by the two letterforms creates a face-like or skull-like silhouette — hollow, eyeless, instantly recognizable once seen. This is the logo's quiet signature: a horror reference embedded in the letterform itself, visible to the attentive eye but not announced. It is the kind of detail that rewards familiarity.

Beneath the wordmark sits the skull/brain emblem — a compact silhouette that reads as a brain from above but resolves into a skull at smaller sizes. The emblem grounds the wordmark and functions as a standalone mark in contexts where the full logo cannot fit.

Together, the wordmark and emblem communicate that ScaryMoovies is horror-first, film-literate, and designed for an audience that takes the genre seriously — not a platform that wears horror as a costume.

## Variants

There are two approved logo variants. No other color treatments exist.

**Black variant** (`/brand/logo-black.png`) uses black letterforms and a black emblem. Use this variant on white or very light neutral backgrounds. It is the less common application — the brand is dark-first, and most surfaces will not be light enough for this variant.

**White variant** (`/brand/logo-white.png`) uses white letterforms and a white emblem. This is the primary variant. Use it on all dark backgrounds: Void Black (`#0c0a0e`), deep surface variants, cinematic dark backgrounds, and any dark UI surface. When the logo appears on photography or textured backgrounds, ensure sufficient contrast before placing it.

Do not attempt to create tinted, reversed, or color-matched variants outside of these two. The logo does not appear in red, grey, or any other brand color.

---

## Clearspace

Maintain a minimum clearspace of **1× the cap-height of the "S"** on all four sides of the logo. Nothing — no type, no UI element, no image edge, no other graphic — enters this zone. The clearspace applies equally to the wordmark-plus-emblem configuration and to the emblem used alone.

When in doubt, give the logo more room. The mark is meant to be seen clearly, not surrounded.

---

## Minimum Size

**Digital:** Do not reproduce the full wordmark at less than **120px wide**. Below this threshold the distressed texture becomes noise, the double-O detail is lost, and the emblem reads as a smudge rather than a mark.

For the emblem used alone, the minimum is **32px wide** — the size at which the skull/brain silhouette still reads with enough clarity to be intentional.

---

## Approved Backgrounds

The logo may appear on the following brand-palette backgrounds:

- **Void Black** `#0c0a0e` (black-500) — primary surface, white variant
- **Deep Surface** `#0b090d` (black-600) — slightly deeper layered surface, white variant
- **Crimson** `#751111` (red-500) — use with caution; only the white variant, only when the context is explicitly brand-promotional rather than functional UI
- **Off-White** `#fefefe` (white-50) — black variant only

Do not place the logo on mid-tone backgrounds, gradient fills, or any surface color not listed above. Avoid placing it directly on photography unless the underlying area is dark, flat, and tested for contrast.

---

## Dos

- Use the white variant on all dark surfaces — it is the correct default
- Use the black variant on white or very light backgrounds when that context is appropriate
- Maintain the lockup: wordmark above, emblem centered below
- Maintain the original proportions of the artwork at all times
- Give the logo its full clearspace in every application
- Download logo files from the approved asset library — do not recreate the mark

---

## Don'ts

- Do not rotate or tilt the logo at any angle
- Do not stretch, compress, or distort the proportions of the wordmark or emblem
- Do not recolor the logo — no red versions, no grey versions, no color-matched treatments
- Do not add drop shadows, glows, outlines, or any other layer effects to the logo
- Do not place the logo on a busy, textured, or photographic background without a solid dark panel behind it
- Do not separate the wordmark from the emblem in official or persistent contexts — the full lockup is required wherever the brand is formally identified
- Do not redraw, trace, or approximate the logo; only use the provided artwork files
- Do not reduce the logo below the minimum sizes

---

## Logo vs. Emblem Only

The skull/brain emblem may be used as a standalone mark in specific limited contexts: **app icons**, **browser favicons**, **social media avatars**, and **small-format brand stamps** where the full wordmark would be unreadable.

In all other contexts — headers, footers, marketing materials, email signatures, press assets, merchandise — use the full wordmark-and-emblem lockup. The emblem alone does not replace the logo; it is an accommodation for size constraints, not a preferred alternative.

When using the emblem alone, the same clearspace and minimum size rules apply. The same two color variants — black and white — are the only approved treatments.
